How To Use This Lab

Controls

Start or pause the simulation, switch boundary modes, turn the educational overlay on, and record a trial. Time scale, gravity, convection, trails, and vector overlays are grouped here so motion controls stay in one place.

Scenario

Build either an open tray or a cup experiment, pick the cup material, set the room conditions, and choose the starting inside temperature for the sample. Use Reset To Ice only when you want to force an ice-start comparison.

Temperature

The inside source, outside temperature, humidity, brush temperature, and unit changes are grouped here. If you change the inside temperature during a run, the sample shifts toward the new value gradually rather than jumping instantly.

Molecules

Particle count, epsilon, sigma, and dt are grouped with particle tracking so the structure of the molecular model stays together in one tab.

Environment

Outside temperature and the environmental heat coefficient control how strongly the surroundings warm or cool the sample. Ambient humidity changes dew point, condensation, and evaporation. Cup thickness changes how hard it is for heat to move through the wall. In cup mode, the material conductivity, wall thickness, outside temperature, and humidity all affect what happens.

Advanced Controls

Gravity settles the sample, convection lets warmer particles rise, trails show recent motion, and vector overlay draws velocity arrows. Use these when students are ready for a deeper look at the motion.

Phase Behavior

At very low temperature the particles stay close and vibrate in place like a solid. At moderate temperature they flow like a liquid. At high temperature they spread out and move faster like a gas.

Temperature Controls

In this model, the sample begins at the chosen inside temperature, then its temperature changes because heat moves through the cup wall and open top toward the room. Changing the inside value during a run acts like changing the source condition, so the molecules respond over time instead of teleporting to a new temperature.

Condensation And Drips

When the outside cup wall drops below the dew point of the room air, water condenses into droplets on the outside surface. Those droplets can merge, slide, detach, and fall. If the wall warms back up or the air is dry, the droplets evaporate away again.

Physics Sources

Water heat capacity, steam heat capacity data, boiling point, and phase-change behavior are based on NIST and USGS references. Cup-wall conductivity values are based on published NIST values for expanded polystyrene and stainless steel, a NIST Pyrex value for glass, and a reported polypropylene value for the plastic cup model.

Time And Units

The simulation now runs in real time at 1x. You can slow it down or speed it up with the time-scale menu. Unit controls let students switch between Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin, plus metric or imperial style readouts for thickness and energy.
